  1. English Summary

In 2022, Armenia experienced a significant increase in exports to the Eurasian Economic Union (EAEU) and the Middle East, nearly tripling its export figures. A substantial portion of these exports constituted re-exports, which are not officially recorded in statistics. To estimate the true volume of re-exports, a method involving comparing six-digit codes of commodities on a monthly basis was employed. According to this methodology, approximately 27.7% of Armenias exports in 2022, equivalent to 1.47 billion drams, were considered re-exports. When considering only net exports, there was a 37% increase in real exports, compared to an official export index increase of 79.0% in 2022.

The majority of Armenias re-exports were directed towards the EAEU and the Middle East, with 41.5% of exports going to the EAEU and 40.8% to the Middle East in 2022. Russia and the United Arab Emirates received the largest volumes of re-exports. Notably, around 41.5% of Armenias exports to Russia in 2022 were re-exports. Without considering re-exports, the net export to Russia almost doubled, showing a real growth rate of approximately 92%.

The main categories of re-exports included equipment, machinery, and industrial goods sent to Russia, primarily originating from European countries, China, the USA, Japan, and South Korea. This was influenced by sanctions imposed on Russia, making it difficult for third countries to export similar goods to Russia. Additionally, exports to the EAEU increased nearly threefold in 2022.

Armenia also re-exported precious and semi-precious stones and metals, particularly diamonds and gold, to the United Arab Emirates. These products primarily came from Russia. In 2022, a significant outflow of capital and labor from Russia led to increased exports to the Middle East, almost tripling the figures.
