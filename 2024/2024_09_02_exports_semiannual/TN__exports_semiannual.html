Gold Rush 2: Gold and Diamonds are 75% of Armenias Exports

Armenias trade dynamics have undergone notable shifts, with exports surging by 55.3% and imports by 40.2% over the last two and a half years. This growth is chiefly driven by the re-export of precious metals, particularly gold, which comprised a third of total exports in 2023. The trend continued into March 2024, with re-exported gold accounting for a significant portion of Armenias export income, notably to the United Arab Emirates and Hong Kong. While this surge has seemingly improved Armenias trade balance, the economys reliance on external factors, particularly the gold rush, poses substantial risks, as any decline in gold re-exports could leave the economy vulnerable.

In the first quarter of 2024, Armenia witnessed a remarkable increase in exports, primarily driven by the re-export of gold, which constituted over three-quarters of total exports. However, this growth masks underlying vulnerabilities, as evidenced by declines in exports of traditional goods and sectors. While the surge in economic activity appears positive, it is heavily contingent on the buoyancy of the gold market, leaving Armenia exposed to external shocks. Thus, while the economic indicators suggest growth, the sustainability of this trajectory hinges on diversifying export sources and reducing reliance on the volatile gold market to mitigate potential risks.
